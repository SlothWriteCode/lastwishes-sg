What's inside

A short, practical checklist you can fill in at your own pace:

  • Where your family stands right now — early planning, comparing options, someone seriously ill, or after a cremation. Each stage needs different things.
  • A family wishes record — religion or belief preference, rituals that matter, things to avoid, who should be involved.
  • Ashes and resting-place options, side by side — home, public columbarium, private columbarium, temple/church, sea or inland scattering — and the right-fit questions to compare them fairly.
  • Price and transparency questions — what's included, what's not, fees, GST, payment terms, transfer rules — the questions to ask before paying anyone, including me.
  • A family conversation script — gentle wording for raising the topic when it feels pantang or too early.

No pressure — a promise, not a slogan

This is not a sales form. Reading the checklist does not commit you to anything, and nobody will chase you with calls.

Many families tell me the same thing after a loss: "I was at a loss on what to do." The whole point of this checklist is that your family never has to feel that way. Fill it in privately, discuss it over dinner, keep it in a drawer — it is yours.

If you do reach out, a real person (me) replies personally on WhatsApp. My first job is to help you understand which category of option fits your family — public, private, home, or scattering. Prices and packages come later, and only if you ask.
