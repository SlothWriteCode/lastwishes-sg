# Singapore Resting-Place Options — sourced landscape

_Date: 2026-07-04 · Purpose: ground the site's "compare fairly" content in facts so the owner (a Nirvana agent) can honestly point families to public/other options without vouching for competitors. Figures are as-of-2026 from the cited sources — always re-verify with official sources before quoting to a family._

## 1. Public / government columbaria (NEA-managed)

- **Locations:** Mandai Columbarium (300 Mandai Road), Choa Chu Kang Columbarium (within CCK Cemetery Complex), Yishun Columbarium. Niches for sale are currently at **Mandai** and **Choa Chu Kang**.
- **Price:** **S$500** standard niche · **S$900** family niche · **+S$250** if you want to choose a specific location within the columbarium.
- **Booking:** online via the **NEA e-Portal** (or in person / by phone). Needs the **death certificate** + applicant ID.
- **Key fact (honest differentiator for pre-planning):** government niches **can only be bought AFTER cremation — there is no advance purchase.** So a family that wants to *plan ahead*, choose a specific spot, or lock in today's price **cannot** do that with a public niche. That's a genuine, factual reason some families choose a private option — not a knock on public options.

## 2. Private columbaria

- **Price range:** roughly **S$3,000 to S$200,000+** depending on facility, niche type, and location.
- **Nirvana Memorial Garden** (950 Old Choa Chu Kang Road) — the largest, best-known private columbarium; operates on a **99-year land lease (to 2098)**. _(This is the owner's own affiliation.)_
- **Temple options:** **Lin San Temple** (17 Lorong 12 Geylang) offers **freehold** niches (no lease expiry); **Kong Meng San** (Bright Hill) — large Buddhist temple offering columbarium services.
- **Lease periods** commonly offered: 30, 40, 99 years, or permanent/freehold.
- **Why families pick private (honest, non-price):** pre-planning ahead of need, choosing location, environment/landscaping, guided services and rituals, family/double niches, managed remembrance. Price is *not* the pitch.

## 3. Ash scattering (secular, open to all faiths)

- **Inland scattering** — only **two** designated NEA facilities:
  - **Garden of Peace** (Choa Chu Kang) — **S$320**.
  - **Garden of Serenity** (Mandai North) — newest facility.
- **Sea scattering** — permitted with **no permit required**; a dedicated ceremony site sits off **Pulau Semakau** (south-west of Sentosa). Cost ranges **~S$80–S$1,000** depending on ritual/boat.

## 4. Keeping ashes at home

- Permitted. Some families keep ashes at home before deciding, or long-term.

## 5. The after-death process (for "urgent" leads)

- Death registered within 24 hours; doctor certifies cause of death online (no counter visit for a natural/expected death).
- Next-of-kin download the **digital death certificate** via **MyLegacy (Singpass)** within 30 days — it comes **bundled with the cremation/burial permit** needed to book a slot.
- Miss the 30-day window → apply for a paid "death extract".
