# WhatsApp Reply Toolkit + Lead Log

_Purpose: de-risk the single biggest failure point — slow or cold replies waste every dollar of ad spend. Set these up in **WhatsApp Business** (free) so leads get a calm, fast, consistent reply even when you're busy._

## The rules (keep them simple)
1. **Reply within the hour during the day.** Speed beats perfect wording. A fast "hi, got your message, give me a moment" keeps the lead warm.
2. **Never open with a sales pitch.** First reply = help + reassurance.
3. **Log every real inbound** (see the log below) — a WhatsApp *message received* is a real lead; an ad click is not.
4. **One question at a time.** These families are stressed; don't overwhelm.

## WhatsApp Business setup (once)
- Business profile: name, hours, short description ("Calm, no-pressure family funeral & columbarium planning · Nirvana agent · Singapore").
- **Greeting message** (auto-sends on first contact) — see below.
- **Away message** for after hours.
- **Quick replies** (saved shortcuts) for the templates below.

## Templates (paste-ready — adjust the wording to sound like you)

### Greeting / auto-reply (first contact)
> Hi, thanks for reaching out 🙏 You've reached [Name], I help Singapore families plan calmly and I'm a Nirvana Memorial Garden agent. No pressure and no obligation here — tell me a little about your situation whenever you're ready, and I'll help you understand your options. If it's urgent, just say "urgent" and I'll reply as fast as I can.

### They messaged "Checklist"
> Happy to share it 🙏 Here's the free family checklist — yours to keep, nothing to sign: [checklist link]. It helps you record wishes and compare options calmly. If any part is confusing, just tell me your situation (early planning / comparing / urgent) and I'll help you make sense of it. No pressure at all.

### They asked about a tour
> I'd be glad to show you around 🙏 The tour is free, private, and there's no obligation to decide anything. Before we pick a time — is this for planning ahead, or is something more urgent? And roughly which part of Singapore are you coming from? I'll work around what's easy for your family.

### They asked about price (reframe — don't quote blindly)
> Good question. Before numbers, it helps to compare the right *type* of option for your family — public, private, home, or scattering — because they're very different. Can I ask: is this planning ahead, or urgent? And any religion/ritual needs? Then I can give you pricing that actually fits, instead of a confusing figure. (For public/government niches, those are booked via NEA after a cremation — I can explain that too.)

### Urgent / someone has just passed
> I'm sorry for your loss 🙏 Let's take the next step calmly. In Singapore the death is certified by a doctor and the family downloads the digital death certificate via MyLegacy (Singpass) — it comes with the permit needed to book cremation. Tell me where things stand right now and I'll help you with the very next step. Take your time.

### Gentle follow-up (only ONE, a day or two later, if they went quiet)
> No pressure at all — just leaving the door open 🙏 Whenever you or your family are ready, I'm here. And if now isn't the time, that's completely okay.
