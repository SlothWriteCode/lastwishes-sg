# Plan Audit — what's wrong / risky before we spend

_Date: 2026-07-04 · A deliberately critical review of the whole plan (site + offer + ads + tracking + SEO). Ranked by how badly it could cost money or sink the effort._

## 🔴 Critical — fix/answer before spending a dollar

### 1. The kill-criteria numbers aren't anchored to your actual economics
The tracking plan sets "cost per lead > ~S$40–50 → kill". **That number is a guess, because we never established what a customer is worth to you.**
- If a Nirvana sale earns you, say, S$1,500–3,000 commission, and you close ~1 in 15–25 leads, you can afford **S$60–150+ per lead** — so a S$50 kill line would wrongly kill a *profitable* channel.
- If commission is small or close rate is low, S$50 might already be too high.
**Need from you:** rough **commission per sale** + a guess at **close rate**. Then the kill thresholds become real, not arbitrary. Until then, every "is this working?" judgement is unanchored.

### 2. Are you actually allowed to market Nirvana this way?
This is the biggest un-checked risk. Many columbarium companies have **strict agent rules** about:
- running your own website/brand,
- using the **"Nirvana" name** in ads/domains (trademark),
- capturing and handling leads yourself.
If your agent agreement forbids independent advertising or use of the brand name, this whole site could breach it (or trademark law). **Confirm with Nirvana/your agreement before running ads.** Cheaper to ask now than to get shut down after spending.

### 3. "Book a free private tour" as the PRIMARY cold-ad CTA is probably too big an ask
A stranger who just Googled a columbarium term is **cold** — asking them to commit to physically visiting a specific private columbarium on first contact is high-friction and emotionally heavy. Cold traffic usually needs the **low-commitment** entry first (checklist / one WhatsApp question), *then* the tour as a step-up.
- Risk: leading tour-first tanks conversion and burns budget.
- Worse: an **urgent, grieving** searcher ("what to do when someone dies") hitting a "book a tour" pitch is a jarring, insensitive mismatch.
**Fix:** for cold ads, lead with the checklist/question; make the tour the second step. Match landing page to search intent (urgent → process/checklist page, not tour).

### 4. The plan quietly depends on YOUR fast, warm WhatsApp follow-through
Every lead is a WhatsApp message you must answer quickly and well. **This is the single biggest point of failure, and it's your known weak spot (follow-through).** No amount of ad spend survives slow/cold replies. The plan says "set an auto-reply" — that's not enough.
**Fix:** a concrete reply routine (saved templates, a within-the-hour rule, the checklist link ready to paste). If you can't commit to replying fast, ads will waste money no matter what.

## 🟠 Important — will distort your results if ignored

### 5. A WhatsApp click is NOT a lead — you'll measure the wrong thing
`wa.me` click tracking counts people who *clicked to open* WhatsApp — not people who actually *sent* a message. Many click and never send. So "cost per WhatsApp lead" from Ads/GA4 **overstates** real leads.
**Fix:** manually log real inbound messages and reconcile against ad clicks weekly. The true metric is messages *received*, which only you can see.

### 6. The fail-fast thresholds are statistically underpowered
"S$60 / 30 clicks, 0 leads → pause." At a realistic 5–15% opt-in, 30 clicks *expects* only ~1.5–4.5 leads — getting 0 could easily be noise, not proof of failure. You'd kill a fine campaign on a small sample.
**Fix:** gather **~50–100 clicks** before judging a keyword/ad. Pause obviously-broken things early, but don't call statistical failure on 30 clicks.

### 7. Sequencing: don't build SEO content for an unvalidated offer
The plan runs ads + SEO somewhat in parallel. But if the offer doesn't convert, writing 5 articles is wasted effort (the Cultural Walk trap).
**Fix:** **validate the offer with the paid test FIRST.** Only invest SEO/content hours once ads prove people actually opt in. Ads = cheap, fast truth; SEO = expensive to build on a wrong offer.

## 🟡 Minor — known gaps, already on the list
- **Sendable checklist PDF** still doesn't exist (auto-reply can send the web link as a stopgap).
- **Intent-matched landing pages** — one page for all keywords hurts Quality Score + conversion.
- **Real domain + UEN** pending; do a quick **trademark/name check** on "Family Wishes SG".
- **Ad copy honesty** — must disclose Nirvana affiliation in ads too, not just on the site.

## ✅ What's solid (don't second-guess these)
- Honest Nirvana-agent positioning (fixed the misleading "independent" wording).
- Non-price / calm strategy — genuinely differentiated in a price-shouting market.
- The pre-planning-is-private-only hook — factual and strong.
- Google **Search** ads are policy-clear for this niche (verified).
- Research is sourced to official bodies; scam guide is defamation-safe.
- Face-free trust approach is viable.

## The one-line verdict
The **creative, positioning, and content are strong**. The **risks are commercial and operational, not design**: (a) you haven't confirmed you're *allowed* to do this as a Nirvana agent, (b) the kill-criteria aren't tied to your real economics, (c) the tour-first CTA likely mismatches cold traffic, and (d) success hinges on *your* WhatsApp follow-through. Resolve 1–4 before spending.
