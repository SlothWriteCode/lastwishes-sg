# Confirm You're Allowed to Market This Way (do before spending)

_Fixes audit risk #2 — the biggest un-checked one. Cheap to clear now; expensive to ignore. 15 minutes with whoever manages your Nirvana agent relationship._

> **OWNER DECISION (2026-07-04): skipping the upfront permission check — proceeding at own risk.** Kept here for reference and, importantly, the **contingency plan** below in case the agent-agreement/brand issue ever surfaces. Note the site is already in the lower-risk posture: neutral brand "Family Wishes SG", **no Nirvana logo or photos**, only a plain-text mention that the owner is a Nirvana agent.

## Ask Nirvana / check your agent agreement

- [ ] Am I allowed to run **my own website / brand** ("Family Wishes SG") to generate columbarium leads?
- [ ] Can I **use the "Nirvana" name** on my site, in ads, and in a domain? (Trademark — usually needs permission.)
- [ ] Can I use **Nirvana photos / videos / logo**? (Likely restricted — that's why the site currently uses none.)
- [ ] Are there **rules on how I advertise** (Google Ads, wording, claims, price mentions)?
- [ ] Can I **capture and hold leads myself** (WhatsApp), or must leads go through the company?
- [ ] Is there **approved marketing language** I must use, or claims I must avoid?
- [ ] Do I need marketing **approved before it goes live**?

## Green light → proceed
If they're fine with it, get it **in writing** (even a WhatsApp confirmation), then launch.

## Amber / red light → the contingency (still workable)
If you may **not** use the "Nirvana" name or advertise independently, you don't lose the work — you pivot the positioning:

- **Drop the brand name, keep the service.** Position as a general **"Singapore family funeral & columbarium planning helper"** who guides families through *all* options (public, private, scattering) — then bring suitable families to Nirvana through the company's approved channel.
- The **checklist, scam guide, options landscape, calm design, and non-price angle all still work** — none of them depend on naming Nirvana.
- Only two things would change: remove "Nirvana agent" wording, and route serious leads via the company's approved process instead of your own tour.

## Why this order
Confirming permission first means: if the answer is "no, not the Nirvana name," you find out **before** buying a domain, running ads, or building SEO around a brand you can't use. One conversation protects everything downstream.
